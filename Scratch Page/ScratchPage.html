A Guide to Handling Flight Delays

This page provides practical, easy-to-follow information for travelers dealing with flight delays.

Common Causes of Flight Delays

  • Severe weather conditions (storms, snow, fog)
  • Mechanical or maintenance issues
  • Air traffic congestion
  • Crew scheduling or availability problems
  • Security or safety concerns

Steps to Take When Your Flight Gets Delayed

  1. Confirm the delay.
    • Check airport monitors or your airlineâ€™s mobile app for official updates.
  2. Identify the cause.
    • Ask a gate agent whether the delay is due to weather, maintenance, or crew issues.
  3. Evaluate rebooking options.
    • Look for alternate flights, connections, or nearby airports.
  4. Speak with airline staff.
    • Ask about rebooking, standby options, or compensation when applicable.
  5. Request accommodations.
    • For long delays, inquire about meal vouchers or hotel arrangements.
  6. Notify contacts.
    • Inform family, friends, or employers about your updated arrival time.
  7. Stay prepared.
    • Keep essentials accessible and continue monitoring updates.

How to Prepare for Possible Delays

  • Pack essentials in your carry-on bag
  • Download airline and travel apps
  • Allow extra time between connecting flights
  • Consider travel insurance for long trips
